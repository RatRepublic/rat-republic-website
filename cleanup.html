The Concept

On the Solana blockchain, every individual token account requires a small "rent" deposit of approximately 0.00203928 SOL to exist. If you've held tokens that were eventually sold, rugged, or migrated, those empty accounts are still holding your SOL hostage. This utility identifies those abandoned, zero-balance accounts and shuts them down, instantly returning the locked rent to your main wallet.

Security & Trust

Your assets remain under your control.

  • Manual Approval: Every action requires your direct signature via your wallet provider (like Phantom).
  • Zero-Balance Protection: The system is programmed to only interact with accounts that are completely empty. If an account holds even a fraction of a token, it is ignored.
  • Non-Custodial: No tokens are ever transferred out of your possession. The only cost is the negligible Solana network gas fee (approx. 0.000005 SOL).
Frequently Asked Questions
Does this impact my eligibility for airdrops?

Absolutely not. Airdrops are tied to your primary wallet address, not specific sub-accounts. If a project sends you tokens in the future, the network simply generates a fresh token account for you. Cleaning up old accounts has no bearing on your future rewards.

Why is my "claimable" balance showing as zero?

The tool specifically filters for accounts with a balance of exactly 0. Many rug pulls or failed projects leave behind "dust" (tiny fractions of a token). If an account holds even 0.000001 of a token, it is considered active and will not be displayed for closure to ensure your safety.

What are the costs?

A 2% service fee is deducted from the recovered rent in the same transaction. If you joined via a referral link, the fee is split: 1.5% to Rat Republic and 0.5% to your referrer. You also pay the standard Solana network transaction fee (usually 0.000005 SOL).

SOL Account Reclamation

Please read this carefully before proceeding.

Irreversible Action

The process of reclaiming SOL by closing empty token accounts or associated accounts is permanent and non-reversible. Once the transaction is confirmed on the blockchain, the account is burned and cannot be "un-closed."

Loss of Access

By reclaiming the rent deposit, you are permanently deleting the account entry for that specific token. If you receive that same token again in the future, a new account will need to be created (which will require a new rent deposit).

Verify Before You Burn

Ensure that the account you are closing is truly "dust" or empty. Closing an account that still contains active tokens or represents a necessary smart contract state may result in the total loss of those assets.

No Support Recovery

Because this is a decentralized on-chain action, no developer, wallet provider, or protocol can "undo" this transaction or recover SOL sent to a burn address.

By proceeding, you acknowledge that you are solely responsible for verifying the accounts you choose to close and that you understand the finality of this action.
